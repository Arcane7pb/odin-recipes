Pasta Carbonara

Description

The truly original recipe for Pasta Carbonara. Pasta Carbonara is a classic Roman dish made with pasta, eggs, cheese, guanciale and black pepper.

The dish is creamy, salty and peppery. With crispy guanciale that melts into your mouth. And makes the flavours explode in your mouth.

Ingredients for 2

  • 200g of pasta of choice
  • 100g of guanciale
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 50g of Pecorino or Parmigiano

Steps

  1. Put 2L of water to boil in a pot.
  2. Cut the guanciale in little cubes
  3. In a pan add your guanciale and cook it in low. Try to cook it evenly and don't let it get burnt. The objective is to have it crispy by the time the pasta is done.
  4. When the water starts boiling add salt, don't be afraid to put too much into it. When boiling pasta we want the water to be as close as sea water.
  5. Look how long does it take for your pasta to get to al dente, once it is boiling once again add the pasta to the water and start your timer.
  6. Split your yolks from the whites, if you haven't already.
  7. Whisk the yolks, add the Pecorino and some pepper to your liking, whisk again.
  8. When the pasta is done save some water for later and add it to the pan, combine the guanciale and its' oils with the pasta. Add the mix of yolks and cheese and combine it again. You may add some water now if the sause isn't creamy beware not to add too much.
  9. Finish the dish by plating the pasta, adding some cheese to the top and a touch of black pepper.
