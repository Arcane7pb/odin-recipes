Spanish Omelette

Description

In Spain, this dish is called tortilla de patatas, which basically means potato omelette. The name is quite self explanatory, potato and egg, that is all you need to make one.

By this point, many Spaniards will come out and say that tortilla de patatas is cooked with onion. Truth is that the authentic tortilla de patatas was just made with potato. However, nowadays a bigger side of the Spanish population cooks it with onion.

We will stick to the original recipe, but we must say that onion is a good addition, and many other ingredients for that matter. So basically our recipe works as a kind of base for many other omelettes in Spain, although some Spanish omelettes are also devoid of potato.

As you just read, Spanish Omelette and the ecosystem around it is quite complex and extensive, with lots of debates and opinions. We recommend keeping it simple and sticking from 2 and up to 4 ingredients overall.

Ingredients

  • 3 or 4 eggs (depends on the variety)
  • Around 300 grams of potato

*As mentioned above we are following the original recipe, in which there was no onion.

*Another mention to try different ingredients, and also removing the potato if needed to stick to the 2 to 4 ingredients rule

Steps

  1. Cut the potatoes in cubes of around 1cm long edges.
  2. Fry the potatoes with low-medium heat until completely cooked inside. The outside should look golden with the borders slightly charred. You can think about french fries anc cook them like that if you prefer.
  3. Set the potatoes aside and let them cool.
  4. Beat the eggs ,add the potatoes and mix everything.
  5. Choose a pan, it should be big enough so all the content fits but small enough so that the mixture has some volume.
  6. Add a touch of oil in the pan, preferably olive oil, don't heat it yet. This should prevent the omelette from sticking to the pan.
  7. Add the mixture to the pan, and heat the fire up. Again low-medium heat.
  8. With a cooking tool even the upper side of the omelette, you can also do this by gently shaking the pan.
  9. You should stick to this side until you see a third to half the content cooked (depends if you like it more or less runny), be careful not to burn the base, this shouldn't happen with the low-medium heat temperature but sometimes it depends on the stove.
  10. Once the omelette reached the desired threshold, put a plate on the pan with the food-contact surface facing the omelette. Press with some strength, to avoid spilling the content of the pan, and turn the pan upside down to pour the content on the plate.
  11. Now starting from the edge of the pan, start pouring the content back to it accompanying with the plate.
  12. Cook this side about as long as it took you to cook the other side with the same heat, after that you can plate your dish.

Now you can enjoy your tortilla de patatas, normally we serve it in triangles, like a pizza, but if there's a lot of people eating we cut it in rectangles.
